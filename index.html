Planet Zoo Helper

This is a fan made tool to aid players with planning habitats in the game.
It is in no way associated with Frontier Developments or the Planet Zoo team.
The tool is free to use.

If you notice any bugs or mistakes on the website, please let me know!
Note: animals are still being added.

The following features are currently available:

Zoopedia

A filterable list of all animals in-game with various data related to them.

▶ Click for more details ◀ You can filter by:
Habitat/Exhibit, DLC, continents, biomes, conservation status (IUCN)
Barrier and minimum habitat area requirements
Temperature, plant coverage, terrain type ranges
Whether guests can enter the habitat or interact with the animal
Whether the animal is a predator, can jump, climb, swim or use burrows
Filter by taxonomy (Class, Order, Family, Genus)
You can also sort alphabetically, by base species appeal, life expectancy or by food cost (for one adult per feed)

You can use a combination of filters to get ideas for which animals you can house together or who to build your next habitat for.
Animals can be mixed in a habitat even if they do not get an interspecies bonus for it, although try to not mix with predators.
This can also be used to get a better idea of what animals to use for certain community challenges.

Animal Cards

Click on any animal's image or icon and it will open up their animal card with more detailed information.
Technically there is also an album feature in there, planned for displaying colormorphs.
You can see an example with the Aardvark.

Habitat Calculator

A tool to help you check habitat requirements and the compatibility of the animals you add.

▶ Click for more details ◀ What will be calculated:
Temperature and plant coverage ranges
Continents and biomes compatible to every added animal
Barrier grade, height and whether it needs to be climb proof
Minimum area requirements for land, climbing, water and deep water depending on the amount of animals added
Terrain ranges required for the animal combination
Food costs per feed depending on the amount of animals added and selected food grade
You will also get various notes regarding what you add:
Whether an animal is a predator (shouldn't be mixed with others)
Whether guests can enter a habitat or interact with certain animals
How many offspring can the females you added have in their lifetime
A note if there's no match for continents/biomes, but animals don't need plant coverage
Notes regarding who can breed (only alpha male/female/couple)
How many adults and what combination of males/females you can have
ETC.

You can use this to see if the animals you added can be mixed together without issues.
If there's issues in any areas, there will be notes like "No Match" in red.
Use this in combination with the Zoopedia to get more ideas.
Note that the animal icons can be clicked to see more information about the animal (like who they get interspecies bonus from).

Zoo Planner

This is basically built with all the calculator functions, but lets you name, save, edit and delete your combos.
The data gets saved in your device's local storage, you can only see what you saved on that specific device.
